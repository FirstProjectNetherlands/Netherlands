Netherlands

In a Nutshell

"God created the world but the Dutch created the Netherlands“

6 Place Happy
The Netherlands is the sixth happiest country

According to the 2020 World Happiness Report. The annual study ranks 156 countries, taking into account factors such as healthy life expectancy, freedom, trust, corruption, and social support.

Bridges 1200
Amsterdam alone has over 1,200 bridges

Bridges are a staple in Amsterdam, thanks to the canals and waterways that take up most of the city. Some bridges are historic, some are lit up at night, and all are beautiful sights to see.

CITIES
More than 26%
Of the country’s territory is below sea level

55 per cent of the country’s territory is located below sea level. There is an extensive system in place that keeps the country safe. Through a complex system of dikes, pumps, the Netherlands stays above water.

Windmills 1000
There are over 1,000 windmills in the Netherlands

Many of Windmills are open to visitors throughout the year incluing a museum and inviting guests to peek inside and learn about its history.

COUNTRYSIDE
Of Dutch 56%
The most physically active European country

According to the Euro Barometer, around 56% of Dutch people play sports on a weekly basis, compared to the European average of 40%. The survey sowed that Dutch mainly exercise for fun.

Kilometers 20000
20 000km of fietspaden bicycle paths

The famously flat country boasts 20,000km of fietspaden making cycling in the Netherlands an extremely pleasant activity. And with so many beautiful Dutch cycling routes to choose from, who can blame them?

ACTIVITIES
Walkers 42.000
Walking perfomance ‘Vierdaagse’

Every July the event take place in the Dutch city Nijmegen. This is a walking performance ride where each year 42.000 walkers participate in. For four days people are walking either 30, 40 or 50 kilometers a day.

color Orange
Orange is the national color

The Dutch royal family is officially called ‘the House of Orange-Nassau’ and their traditional color is… orange. The Dutch wear orange on days of national pride, and their sports teams wear bright orange uniforms, too.
